Week 1

Exercise 1.1
The approach for the first section of lab 1 exercise is to try to create something simple of a house and with people to decorate the environment. The reason for such is that as a kid, whenever I learn how to draw or make something, I always start with this basic concept, and since I am learning a new software, I wanted to translate that idea.

Exercise 1.2
In the second exercise, I wanted to create a head sculpture that was abstract in terms of shape which seems to add a bit of movement when the audience looks at it. However, I kept the model to be rather simple during this exploration and learning stage since the higher level techniques have not been learned yet.

Exercise 1.3
With the inspiration of the feature animation film of Frozen from Disney Pixar, I wanted to create a pattern of snowflakes. Furthermore, because of the exercise in making a pattern, I wanted to see how a flat plane will render out if for my team project we decide to model some 2D elements for a 2D world that we have.

Week 2

Exercise 2.1
For this exercise, I wanted to recreate the balance figure of a human character as what it will look like if they were trying to balance on a moving cart.

Exercise 2.2
In the images below, the approach that I went for is to show that the doll figure is really small inside the drawer and how it wants to leave, but then there is a large girl present. With this scene, I also wanted to translate the idea from Toys Story.

Exercise 2.3
As for the environment modelling exercise, the approach that I went with is to create a castle wall that protects people inside and that because it is isolated on a piece of land surrounded by a river, a bridge was build. The first shot is to show the entire layout of the land and the second shot shows a close up to represent the perspective of someone walking on the bridge.

Week 3

Exercise 3.1
In this exercise, I wanted to create cookie cutters that can potentially make cookies that refers to night time, hence the star cutter and the moon cutter that is placed on a plate.

Exercise 3.2
For this beer mug learned from the tutorial, I wanted to explore the camera angles to give a sense of change in perception of how it will look like at a normal distance and how it will be a different contrast when it is extremely zoomed in.

Exercise 3.3
To practice more on manipulating curves for the NURBS curve tools, I decided to make a wine glass and bottle since it will be two of the type of objects needed in my team project animation.
